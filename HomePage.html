KYOTO-OSAKA (JAPAN)

Known as the ‘Land of the Rising Sun’, Japan is known for its mix of ancient samurai traditions with a modern-day persona and new world technology. As a land of contrasts, Japan is a fascinating destination for visitors. The cities of Kyoto and Osaka are widely known for its historical and cultural presence, even today ancient Samurai traditions are still highly respected by the locals and many are still practicing their traditions today.

Sushi, teppanyaki, ramen and more japanese cuisines have won devotees all over the world, and is way more delicious in Japan itself. Most traditional eateries in Kyoto and Osaka serve their traditional dishes seasonally using ultra-fresh ingredients normally caught right from the sea and beautifully plated. Restaurants in Kyoto / Osaka come at all levels from back streets local sushi bars, ramen stalls, to downtown districts home to even worldly recognized restaurants run by celebrity chefs.

Visitors need not worry about communication, as many Japanese people speak some English, and the locals are among the most gracious and respectful on the planet.

SABAH (MALAYSIA)

Located at the northen part of the Borneo Island, Sabah is a perfect getaway in the heart of Borneo for its combination of lush rainforest, majestic mountains, tropical islands, undersea wonders, unique cultures and friendly people

Sabah is the pearl of Borneo with countless ecotourism activities, beach retreats and has almost 400 islands. Tunku Abdul Rahman National Park consists of five islands forming a chain off the coast of Kota Kinabalu. Some of Sabah's most famous islands include the world–famous diving hotspot Pulau Sipadan, he most visited island Pulau Manukan, Pulau Sapi, and Pulau Gaya.

Boasting as SouthEast Asia tallest mountain and a well known world heritage, Mount Kinabalu peak at 4,095 metres (13,435 feet) is also one of the reason to visit Sabah. Climbing to the summit peak will involves staying overnight on the mountain accomodation and spots fill up months in advance. According to traditions, Mount Kinabalu is also a sacred site for the local Kazadan-Dusun ethnic.

SHANGHAI (CHINA)

Shanghai is one of the few cities in the world evoke so much history, excess, glamour, mystique and exotic promise in name alone.

Shanghai is home to the world's second-tallest tower and a host of other neck-craning colossi. But it's not all sky-scraping razzmatazz. Beyond the crisply cool veneer of the modern city typified by Pudong, you can encounter copious architectural styles.

Beijing often hogs the limelight as China’s cultural nexus, but for a town of wheelers and dealers, Shanghai is surprisingly creative. Many art galleries are exciting, offering a window into contemporary Chinese concerns, while nightlife options have exploded. Acrobatics shows are always a tourist favourite and you might grab the chance to catch some Chinese opera. Shanghai’s music and club scenes are vibrant: from unpretentious jazz and indie venues to all-night hip-hop and electro-dance parties, the city swings with the best of them.

PARIS (FRANCE)

Paris, the City of Light, evokes romantic emotions in anyone who has ever visited. This centuries-old city is the most visited city in the world, and for good reason. There is plenty to do in Paris to satisfy travelers of all tastes.

History lovers will marvel at the richness of a city that has seen two major revolutions and was home to many of the most powerful monarchies of Europe. Art lovers could spend a lifetime browsing the galleries of the world famous Louvre museum, the Musee d’Orsay or countless other smaller art museums the city has to offer. The gourmet in you can indulge in literally any type of culinary extasy--Paris has them all in its small bistrots, charming cafes, elegant restaurants and decadent patisseries. Fashion seekers will be in heaven in Paris’ finest department stores and most exclusive boutiques.

PHUKET (THAILAND)

Phuket refers to both the province and the island in southern Thailand. It’s the country’s second smallest province but it’s biggest island, home to beautiful white sand beaches along its southern and western coast and a spattering of smaller islands just off the mainland.

Surrounded by the Andaman Sea and about an hour by plane from Bangkok, this island is a little piece of paradise, which comes with a relatively low price tag for everything from its accommodations to spa treatments and boat tours. Phuket is a tropical island destination so the best time to go is largely dependent on the weather. Phuket is least rainy from around the end of November till mid-April, making this the ideal time to visit.

Friendly people, varied cuisine, tropical climate and range of amenities on this island can give everyone an ideal destination for a holiday or long term stay.

PENGHU COUNTY (TAIWAN)

Locating at the southwest of Taiwan Strait composing of 90 islands, Penghu County has a steeped in history and culture with gorgeous ocean views and various natural wonders.

One of the major element which sculpted Penghu's geological landscape are the world famous hexagonal Basalt columns that surround the entire island. Furthermore, the clear waters of Bays and strong winter winds have put Penghu on the for competitive sailing map as an international hot spot. For tourism, the government host magnificent firework festivals in the spring and summer. In fall and winter, the fresh and delicious catch of the sea at the Penghu Seafood Carnival.

If you like seaside fun with blue seas, clear skies, soft white beaches, plenty of beach and water recreation facilities, and an amazing marine ecology and scenic beauty, Penghu is the one of the best place to visit.
